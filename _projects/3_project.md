## Motion in one dimension

In this book, they use the **particle model** to describe a moving object as a particle regarding of its sizes. 

### Position, velocity and speed

The displacement of a particle is defined as its charge in position in some interval. 
where $$\Delta x \equiv x_f - x_i$$
- $\Delta x$  is displacement
- $x_f$ is final position
- $x_i$ is the initial position

Distance is the length of a path followed by a particle

The average velocity $V_{x,avg}$ of a particle is defined as $V_{x,avg} \equiv \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$
The average speed $V_{x,avg}$ of a particle is defined as $V_{x,avg} \equiv |\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}|$

### Instantaneous velocity and speed

The instantaneous velocity 
$$V_x \equiv \lim_{\Delta t \to \infty} \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t} = \frac{dx}{dt}$$

The instantaneous speed 
$$V_x = |\frac{dx}{dt}|$$

### Acceleration

Suppose an object that can be modeled as a particle moving along the x-axis have a initial velocity $V_{xi}$ at time $t_i$ and a final velocity $V_{xf}$ at time $t_f$

The average acceleration $a_{x,avg} \equiv \frac{\Delta V_x}{\Delta t} = \frac{d V_x}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt} (\frac{dx}{dt})$

That is, in one-dimensional motion, the acceleration equals the second derivative of x with respect to time.
